Key Labelling

Section Walk Through

The next step is to define the important bits of your data. Namely, the file name, the target, the amount of columns to be considered and their names. I'll explain a little more.

File Name - Since you can upload multiple files to our database, we need to confirm which file you would like to proceed with.

Target - Also known as the predictor. This is the item you want to predict, find out, analyse! The reason you are here!

Amount of Columns - We need to know how many columns or input variables you would like to work with. It helps us keep everything clean, tells us how many interactive sliders you need later and sets up our filters to help keep your dataset in shape as it moves through the modelling process.

Column Names - This is an important step as it confirms to us, the columns or variables you would like to use. Note - Any varibales within your dataset that are not explicitly named, will be dropped and not processed into the model.

      

      

      

      



      

      



      

      









































Classification Designation

The next step is to define what exactly it is you are trying to classify. Quick refresher, in Binary Classification we are try to determine whether something is one thing or another i.e a coin is either heads or tails?, an exam is either passed or failed etc.

The model will however boil these classes down to either a 0 or a 1 (Hence "Binary"), therefore its our job to assign classes to the 0 and the 1 to help us clearly understand the model output.
